Roommate Problem

Problem

In this section, we explore one category matching. Suppose a group of students will be matched to roommates, two in each room. We consider four students:

  • $A$ prefers $B \succ C \succ D$
  • $B$ prefers $C \succ A \succ D$
  • $C$ prefers $A \succ B \succ D$
Then no stable matching exists: whoever is paired with $D$ wants to change and can find a willing partner, so the existence of a stable matching is not trivial, even if each match inovlves just two people.
