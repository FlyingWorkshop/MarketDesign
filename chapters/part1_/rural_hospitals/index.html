Definition: Rural Hospitals Theorem
The sets of men and women who are unmatched is the same in all stable matchings.

Caution: This does not mean that the matchings are the same, just that the sets of men and women who are matched or unmatched is the same across all stable matchings.

Proof:
Let $M, W$ be the sets of men and women matched in the man-optimal stable matching (which is also woman-pessimal). Let $M', W'$ be the sets of men and women matched in some other stable matching. We seek to show that $M = M'$ and $W = W'$. Any man in $M'$ must also be matched in the man-optimal stable matching, so $M' \subseteq M$ $\ldots$ and also $|M'| \leq |M|$. Similarly, since any woman in $W$ is no worse off in the alternative matching, $W \subseteq W'$ $\ldots$ and also $|W| \leq |W'|$. In any feasible matching, the number of matched men equals the number of matched women, so $|M| = |W|$ and $|M'| = |W'|$. We have: $\left|M'\right|\le\left|M\right|=\left|W\right|=\le\left|W'\right|=\left|M'\right|$, so all these are equal. Adding the set inclusions, we must have some $M=M'$ and $W=W'$. $\blacksquare$
