Two-Sided Matching

Problem Statement

  • Two different kinds of parties to be matched.
  • Participants of both kinds care about to whom they are matched.
  • Money and prices cannot be used to guide the match.

Definition: Matching
A matching is a set of pairs of the form $(m, w)$ of self-matching $(m, m)$ or $(w, w)$ such that each individual appears in exactly one pair.
Notation: If the match includes $(m, m)$, then $m$ is unmatched. Similarly, if $\ldots (w, w) \ldots$ then $w$ is unmatched.
